Curie Trust Financial Control Center — Project Requirements Specification (PRS) v1.7

1) Mission

Build a local-first financial control center for the Curie Trust that continuously answers three questions with high clarity:
	1.	What is our latest net worth (right now)?
	2.	Where did our income go (by top budget category and projects/tags)?
	3.	What rate of return are we getting on our investments (before fees, after fees, after taxes)?
	4.	(New) Will our capital last until age 95?

The system ingests periodic CSV/spreadsheet uploads from multiple financial institutions, normalizes them into Curie Trust’s internal data model, and keeps market values fresh via API “drip” updates.

4.6 Forecast (The Time Machine) - REVISED v1.7
	•	Global Settings:
		•	Birth Year: Used to calculate current age and the horizon line (Age 95).
		•	Inflation Rate %: User entered (default 3.0%). Applies to all expenses annually.
		•	Assumed Portfolio Return %: User entered (default 5.0%). Applies to liquid assets annually.
	•	Base Cost of Living (CoL) Engine:
		•	Selector: Collapsible list of all expense categories.
		•	Calculation: System sums the *last 12 months of actuals* for checked categories to establish "Year 0 Base CoL".
		•	Projection: This base amount grows annually by the Inflation Rate.
	•	Discretionary Budget Table:
		•	Table for future one-off or recurring large expenses (e.g., "World Tour").
		•	"Replicate Forward": Option to repeat an expense annually until age 95, adjusting for inflation.
	•	The Simulation (Runway Logic):
		•	System calculates year-over-year Net Worth:
			`Next_NW = (Current_NW * (1 + Return_Rate)) + (Future_Income - (Base_CoL + Discretionary))`
		•	Real Estate values grow separately by their specific Appreciation Rates.
	•	Visualization (The Runway Graph):
		•	Primary View: A Line Graph of **Projected Total Net Worth** from now until Age 95.
		•	Goal: Visual confirmation of whether assets are exhausted (line hits zero) or sustained (line stays positive).
		•	Secondary View (Tooltip/Overlay): Show the "Annual Deficit/Surplus" for any hovered year.
